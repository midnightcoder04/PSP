## D.I.S.C. — Discovering My Personal Behavioural Design

### WHAT IS THE D.I.S.C. MODEL?

**THE D.I.S.C. MODEL** was developed by Bill Bonnstetter at Target Training International, Phoenix, Arizona. It is based on the psychology of Carl Jung and William Moulton Marston and is widely accepted as an effective tool for enhancing performance in all areas of life.

### UNDERSTANDING THE D.I.S.C. MODEL

Through the combined Nature/Nurture process of growing, you have developed specific ways of interacting with the world. This is your Personal Behavioural Style, or **CORE STYLE**.

The D.I.S.C. model uses four factors to determine behaviour:

| # | Factor | Letter | Description |
|---|--------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **THE DOMINANCE FACTOR** | **D** | How you handle problems and challenges |
| 2 | **THE INFLUENCE FACTOR** | **I** | How you handle people and influence others |
| 3 | **THE STEADINESS FACTOR** | **S** | How you handle and pace yourself |
| 4 | **THE COMPLIANCE FACTOR** | **C** | How you handle the rules set by others |

> *"...When you interact with another person in sync with their PERSONAL BEHAVIORAL DESIGN, you open the door to communication with that person."*
> — **Bill Bonnstetter**

You must know how you interact with others — especially those who do not share your beliefs, values, and personality traits — before you can fully understand yourself. - **QUESTION 1:** Is the person predominantly **EXTROVERTED**? OR predominantly **INTROVERTED**?
- **QUESTION 2:** Is the person predominantly **PEOPLE-ORIENTED**? OR predominantly **TASK-ORIENTED**?

### YOUR COMFORT ZONES

You've heard the phrase "personality conflicts"? Some personality types get along better than others. Comfort Zones are the natural degree to which various personality types get along with each other.

Study the four basic CORE STYLES and their characteristics. Once you have determined another individual's CORE STYLE, study the characteristics of their style and how it interacts with your own. This will enable you to establish the best course of action for interacting (and resolving conflicts) with this individual.

First, let's look at how attitudes affect your values.

In 1928, Eduard Spranger wrote and published **The Types of Men**. Spranger observed that all people view the world differently. Whenever several people viewed the same object/event, they invariably placed different values upon it. Some judged it positively, some negatively, and some were indifferent. Through his research, Spranger identified 6 world views that affect the values people place upon objects or events. He labelled these views as Attitudes.

*(Adapted with permission from How To Read and Understand People Copyright 1988 Target Training International)*

### What is an Attitude?

> *"A state of feeling or state of mind about a person or situation."*
> — **WEBSTER'S DICTIONARY**

Spranger states that an attitude is:

1. a world view, a way of looking at life
2. a paradigm of thought
3. a value or judgment of life
4. a mindset

**Spranger noted that basically:**

1. **VALUES AND ATTITUDES** → create → **WANTS**
2. **WANTS** → determine → **BEHAVIOUR**
3. **BEHAVIOUR** → achieves → **RESULTS**

> *"I will see it when I believe it!"*

We all see the world through our own attitude-tinted eyes. And we either reject or value various aspects of life based upon our attitude towards them.

We all have attitudes and according to Spranger, there is no such thing as a "good" or "bad" attitude.

To summarize, an attitude is a composed set of beliefs. People with same or similar attitudes will have same or similar values. As a result, their motivations and methods for achieving satisfaction and pleasure will vary.

# SETTING GOALS

Welcome back.

Doesn't time fly when you're having fun? Now that you've completed your filters for your goals it's time to **SET** your goals.

Research has shown that people who only have career or financial goals are less likely to achieve them than those who have a comprehensive set of goals that take every aspect of their lives into account. And if these individuals do achieve their career goals, they do not find the satisfaction from them that they anticipated.

Read **"My Life Goal Inventory"** and then begin the exercise. Remember each goal has to pass through the four filters — personality, values, roles, and skills. Take into account the continuous interplay and dynamics created between these four factors.

In the exercise, you will look at how each goal impacts every other goal — whether positively, negatively or neutrally. Then, you will prioritize your goals and set a strategic plan for achieving each one.

## The Importance of Visualization in Achieving Your Goals

Research has shown that every thought we think has an affect on the chemistry of our bodies. By visualizing goal attainment, we actually increase our motivation to achieve them.

**The next exercise will show you how visualization will help you in achieving your goals:**

- ● Stand up.
- ● Take a deep breath and let it out slowly.
- ● Repeat two or three times. If you know other body relaxation exercises, do them now.
- ● Once you are relaxed, close your eyes, get comfortable, and go to a place called the "hallways of your mind". This is a limitless place with an infinite number of rooms and spaces. Can you see it? Find the place that has to do with achieving all 8 of your goals.
- ● Visualize yourself having achieved all 8 of these goals.
- ● Take note of your posture in this situation. Note your breathing pattern and how your muscles feel. Are they tense, relaxed, alert?
- ● Take note if there is anyone else with you in this place. Who are they? What are their posture and facial expressions? How are they relating to you?
- ● Note everything around you in this place, including colours, sounds, textures, smells and tastes. Recognize that you can go to this place any time. And that you can change anything at any time.
- ● Now take a deep breath and blow it out as if you were blowing out a candle.
- ● Open your eyes and take a moment to reorientate yourself to where you are.

Do this exercise at least once a day, twice if you can. The best times are in the morning when you first wake up and at night as you're dozing off to sleep. Go to this place any time you are under stress or have lost track of where you're going and what you are doing in your life. Some find it helpful to use a picture that represents their future achievements. Whether you buy one or draw one yourself, a concrete visual representation can be very powerful.

## My Declaration of Self-Esteem

*(by Virginia Satir, 1970)*

*Virginia Satir is known as the grandmother of family therapy. Truly a pioneer in her field, she is now known around the world through many of her books, and students of her unique method of family therapy.*

- ● I am me.
- ● In all the world, there is no one exactly like me. There are persons who have some parts of me, but no one adds up exactly like me. Therefore, everything that comes out of me is authentically mine because I alone chose it.
- ● I own everything about me — my body, including everything it does; my mind, including all its thoughts and ideas; my eyes, including the images of all they behold; my feelings, whatever they may be — anger, joy, frustration, love, disappointment, excitement; my mouth, and all the words that come out of it, polite, sweet or rough, correct or incorrect; my voice, loud or soft, and all my actions, whether they be to others or to myself.
- ● I own my fantasies, my dreams, my hopes, my fears.
- ● I own all my triumphs and successes, all my failures, and mistakes.
- ● Because I own all of me, I can become intimately acquainted with me. By so doing I can love me and be friendly with me in all my parts. I can then make it possible for all of me to work in my best interest.
- ● I know there are aspects about myself that puzzle me, and other aspects I do not know. But as long as I am friendly and loving to myself, I can courageously and hopefully look for the solutions to the puzzles and for ways to find out more about me.
- ● However I look and sound, whatever I say and do, and whatever I think and feel at a given moment in time is me. This is authentic and represents where I am at that moment in time.
- ● When I review later how I looked and sounded, what I did and said, and how I thought and felt, some parts may turn out to be unfitting. I can discard that which is unfitting, and keep that which proved fitting, and invent something new for that which I discarded.
- ● I can see, hear, feel, think, and do. I have the tools to survive, to be close to others, to be productive, and to make sense and order out of the world of people and things outside of me.
- ● I own me, and therefore I can engineer me. I am me and I am okay.
